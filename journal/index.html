Visual Thinking Analysis Part Two

Analyzing images

February 12, 2026

The image above is from a collection of toys/figures I collect from a series called Dimoo. What makes this image interesting is that each character has unique physical aspects. While the same character is being depicted, each iteration has something unique. Take for example, the third figure has a tree motif. But when you look closer, there is a moss-like texture on the figurine.

As previously stated, this image shows the collection of toys that I collect. On a surface level, this reveals that I like to collect figures that have unique themes but are also cute in appearance. There are aspects of it that look very lively and put me in a good mood when I look at them. While simultaneously, I'm also a consumer and I like the consumption of buying such figures from blind boxes.

However, if I wanted to make my images more visually compelling and narrative focused, I have some ideas. One idea could be placing them beside my school works. What I would want to communicate is that these figures help make my world more lively and positive. Especially when I'm constantly stressed from academics, these figures help to ground me in ways that calm and add liveliness to my mood.

Another idea I have is to take pictures of my figures that would reveal a part of my own personality. For example, from the image above, there is one figure that is sleeping in snow. What I want to communicate is that I love sublimity and nature as the figure best presents that aspect. Each figure can represent a different part of my own personality and what makes me whole.

Modals

What are the best practices for modals?

January 29, 2026

This Medium article discusses the best practices of modals, also known as overlays or dialog windows. Modals sit on top of a page and block interaction with the page’s main content until the user interacts with them.

Common usages with modals are to capture user attention, require user input, and show additional information in context. However, it’s not recommended to use modals to show error, success, or warning messages.

Good modal practices include providing users a way to escape the modal window, such as a close, escape, or cancel button. Another practice is to ensure the modal uses descriptive titles so users know its purpose. It is also important to make sure the content is neither too big nor too small and remains visible within the screen.

Modals can be beneficial but sometimes a hindrance to the user. While they help capture attention and simplify context, they can also disrupt the user’s flow since the main content becomes inaccessible.

Research Form Design

Getting Started

January 19, 2026

This Medium article discusses best practices to follow when designing form pages. For any website or mobile application, the ultimate goal is to encourage users to engage with the application.

A highly critical concept to make forms effective is not making them complex. This can be done through different UX design choices such as showing the number of steps through a progress bar.

Ultimately, most people don’t like filling out forms. That is why it’s best practice for designers to make forms easily accessible and straightforward.
